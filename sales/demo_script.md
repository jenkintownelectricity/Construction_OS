# Detail Atlas — Demo Script

## Narrative Flow (5 minutes)

### 1. Select System (30 sec)
"We start by selecting a manufacturer system. Today we'll use Barrett PMMA — their RamFlash flashing system. The system knows Barrett's products, layer order, and application rules."

### 2. Select Condition (30 sec)
"Now we pick a construction condition — the specific place where the flashing system meets the building. Let's do an equipment curb — the raised frame around rooftop HVAC units."

### 3. Generate Detail (1 min)
"The parametric generator reads the calibration data — slab thickness, curb height, insulation depth — and produces a geometry payload. Every component is positioned according to Barrett's rules: cant strip at the base, PMMA system wrapping up the curb, counter-flashing at top."

### 4. Render Print Sheet (1 min)
"The SVG renderer takes that geometry and produces a print-ready detail sheet. White background, black linework, numbered callouts, dimension lines, titleblock. This is what goes to the architect."

### 5. Export PDF / DXF (1 min)
"One click gives us a PDF for email or print. Another gives us a DXF for CAD — with proper layers so it drops into AutoCAD with SUBSTRATE, MEMBRANE, REINFORCEMENT, METAL layers already separated."

### 6. Scale (1 min)
"We just did one condition. The system has 10 Barrett PMMA conditions ready. Run the generator once and you get a complete 10-page detail packet. Change the manufacturer to GAF or Siplast, swap the assembly DNA template, and the same 10 conditions generate with different products."

## Key Talking Points

- "This is not CAD drafting. This is parametric construction intelligence."
- "The detail knows what products go where. The geometry is generated, not drawn."
- "Change a dimension, regenerate the sheet. No manual redraw."
- "One engine, multiple manufacturers, same condition families."
