# Division 07 Chemistry Alignment

## Purpose

Maps chemistry-domain truth to CSI Division 07 — Building Envelope Systems sections. Chemistry truth crosscuts all Division 07 sections where chemical behavior governs material performance.

## Section-to-Chemistry Mapping

### 07 10 00 — Dampproofing and Waterproofing
- **Chemistry families:** bituminous, polyurethane, epoxy, acrylic, bentonite
- **Key chemistry concerns:** solvent compatibility with substrates, cure in below-grade conditions, chemical resistance to soil chemistry
- **Critical incompatibilities:** bituminous coatings with polystyrene insulation (solvent attack)

### 07 20 00 — Thermal Protection
- **Chemistry families:** polyurethane (SPF), polystyrene, polyisocyanurate
- **Key chemistry concerns:** adhesive compatibility with insulation facers, spray foam cure chemistry (isocyanate + polyol reaction), off-gassing
- **Critical incompatibilities:** solvent-based adhesives on foam insulation (dissolution)

### 07 40 00 — Roofing and Siding Panels
- **Chemistry families:** silicone, polyurethane, butyl
- **Key chemistry concerns:** sealant adhesion to coated metals, galvanic interaction at sealant-metal interface, UV exposure of exposed sealants

### 07 50 00 — Membrane Roofing
- **Chemistry families:** EPDM, TPO (polyolefin), PVC, SBS, APP, PMMA
- **Key chemistry concerns:** membrane welding chemistry (heat/solvent), adhesive compatibility, plasticizer migration in PVC, EPDM-to-TPO incompatibility
- **Critical incompatibilities:** PVC plasticizer migration into polystyrene, EPDM contact with petroleum-based products

### 07 60 00 — Flashing and Sheet Metal
- **Chemistry families:** butyl, silicone, polyurethane
- **Key chemistry concerns:** sealant adhesion to metal substrates, primer requirements for coated metals, galvanic corrosion at dissimilar metal joints

### 07 70 00 — Roof and Wall Specialties
- **Chemistry families:** silicone, polyurethane, acrylic
- **Key chemistry concerns:** UV stability of exposed sealants, adhesion to specialty substrates (GFRC, ACM)

### 07 90 00 — Joint Protection
- **Chemistry families:** silicone, polyurethane, polysulfide, hybrid
- **Key chemistry concerns:** cure mechanism selection for joint conditions, movement capability vs. chemistry, adhesion to dissimilar substrates at transitions
- **Critical incompatibilities:** silicone residue preventing adhesion of subsequent sealants

## Chemistry Crosscutting Concerns

These chemistry facts apply across multiple Division 07 sections:
1. **Primer chemistry** must be compatible with both substrate and sealant
2. **Solvent-based products** near foam insulation require compatibility verification
3. **Moisture-cure products** require minimum humidity and temperature
4. **UV-exposed chemistries** require UV-stable polymer backbone or protective coating
5. **Plasticizer-containing systems** require migration risk assessment at contact boundaries
