Governing Doctrines

  1. Mirrors, not wires. We never expose internal kernel APIs. We project governed reflections.
  2. Slices, not monoliths. Clients take exactly what they need. Nothing more.
  3. Parity, not trust. We do not assume mirrors behave correctly. We measure continuously.
  4. Drift, not failure. Divergence is expected. It is governed, not punished.
  5. Exits, not traps. Every attachment has a clean detachment path. Always.
  6. Promotion, not accumulation. Good ideas flow inward. The kernel improves from every engagement.

1. System Context

Highest-level view showing the Construction OS Core, Mirror Kernel Nucleus, mirror instances for partners and internal use, and external systems beyond the shield boundary. Everything flows through governed reflections.

2. Kernel to Mirror Hierarchy

The layered relationship from Core to Nucleus to Mirror Instances to Slices to Reflections. The Registry tracks every element at every level. This is the structural backbone of the architecture.

3. Slice Attachment Model

Clients attach at the slice level, taking exactly what they need. Five capability slices are available: Governance, Validation, Generation, Engine, and Lineage. Each is independently attachable and detachable.

4. Parity Drift Loop

Every active mirror runs a continuous behavioral comparison loop. Canonical fixtures feed both the kernel and mirror, outputs are compared, and results are classified as PASS, DRIFT, or BREAK. Drift is governed, not punished.

5. Registry Control Plane

The lifecycle state machine governing every mirror: PROPOSED, CHARTERED, STAGED, ACTIVE, FROZEN, RETIRED. The control plane manages slice enablement, parity reviews, drift records, transfer readiness, and promotion reviews.

6. Promotion vs Breakaway

Two governed paths for mirror reflections. Stable, reusable reflections are promoted inward into the kernel core (left path). Incompatible or partner-specific reflections are frozen and detached outward (right path).

7. Transfer & Buyout Path

The clean exit protocol. Slices are classified by transfer eligibility, pass through a detachment gate with IP audit and dependency checks, are packaged into a handoff bundle, and exit cleanly. The partner operates independently.

8. GCP Shop Drawing Mirror Instance

A concrete example showing the GCP Shop Drawing mirror with five active slices: detail_normalization, rules_engine, validation, artifact_manifest, and lineage. Each slice contains reflected contracts, schemas, rules, and fixtures with parity tracking.
